The big issue: your ROI is almost certainly “edge on deployed notional,” not account ROI

Your Total ROI shows +0.03%, with Total P and L +18,514.71 and “Capital Required” 10,000.

If ROI were account-style ROI on required capital, it would be:
	•	18,514.71 / 10,000 = 185.15% (not 0.03%)

So your ROI is being computed with a much larger denominator than 10,000.

And your “Avg P and L per trade” is +0.29 per 1000 position. If you assume each trade deploys ~1,000 notional, then:
	•	Total deployed notional ≈ 63,258 trades × 1,000 = 63,258,000
	•	ROI ≈ 18,514.71 / 63,258,000 = 0.0002926 = 0.029% → displayed as 0.03% ✅

That lines up almost perfectly.

Translation

You’re showing ROI on total traded notional (sum of position sizes) — basically “edge” — while also showing capital required as a separate concept.

That’s not “wrong,” but it’s misleading unless you label it clearly.

One more likely logic bug: what do the time buckets mean?

Given 91+ Days has 45,196 trades, that bucket probably means:
	•	“Trades closed more than 91 days ago” (age since close), not
	•	“Trades held longer than 91 days” (trade duration)

If you intended duration, you’re almost certainly bucketing on the wrong timestamp (using now - entry_time or now - exit_time instead of exit_time - entry_time).

Quick rule
	•	Duration bucket: holding_days = exit_ts - entry_ts
	•	Recency bucket: age_days = now - exit_ts
